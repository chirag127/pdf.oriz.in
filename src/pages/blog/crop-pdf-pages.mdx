## Why Crop PDF Pages?

There are numerous reasons you might need to crop PDF pages. Understanding these use cases will help you recognize when cropping is necessary for your documents.

**Removing Excessive Margins**: Scanned documents often come with large margins that waste space and make reading difficult. Cropping removes these margins to create a more compact, readable document.

**Preparing for Print**: When printing PDFs, large margins can force unwanted page breaks or waste paper. Cropping helps optimize your document for specific paper sizes.

**Enhancing Visual Appeal**: Documents with consistent, properly sized margins look more professional and polished. Cropping ensures uniformity across all pages.

**Extracting Specific Content**: Sometimes you need to isolate a particular section of a document, such as a chart, image, or table. Cropping allows you to focus on that content.

**Archive Optimization**: For digital archives, reducing file size through cropping can help manage storage while maintaining document legibility.

### Crop Box vs. Media Box

PDFs contain several "boxes" that define different aspects of page display. Understanding these boxes helps you make better cropping decisions:

- **Media Box**: The total page dimensions, including all content
- **Crop Box**: The visible area displayed to users
- **Trim Box**: The final trimmed page size (for printing)
- **Bleed Box**: Area that may be trimmed off during printing

When you crop a PDF, you're primarily adjusting the Crop Box, which determines what's visible when viewing or printing the document.
