Your letterhead is one of the most frequently used brand assets in your business. Every official letter, invoice, proposal, and formal communication carries your letterhead — making it a constant representative of your brand identity. A professional PDF letterhead template ensures consistency, saves time, and projects credibility with every document you send.

This guide covers everything from design principles to practical implementation of PDF letterhead templates for businesses of all sizes.

### Typography

Letterhead typography must balance brand expression with readability:

- **Company name**: Brand font or a clean sans-serif, 14-18pt
- **Contact info**: 8-10pt, regular weight, high readability
- **Body font**: Standard letter fonts — Calibri, Arial, Georgia, or Times New Roman at 11-12pt
- **Consistency**: Use the same fonts across all brand materials

### Color and Branding

- **Primary brand color**: Use for logo, headers, or accent elements
- **Secondary colors**: Supporting elements, borders, or dividers
- **Print-safe colors**: Test how your colors reproduce in print
- **Black and white version**: Ensure your letterhead works in grayscale for fax or photocopy

<TipBox title="Branding Rule" type="tip">
Your letterhead should be instantly recognizable as yours. Use the same logo, colors, and typography as your website, business cards, and marketing materials. Consistency builds brand recognition and trust.
</TipBox>

## Creating PDF Letterhead Templates

### Design Workflow

1. **Define dimensions**: Standard letter size (8.5" x 11") or A4 (210mm x 297mm)
2. **Set up margins**: Minimum 0.5" on all sides; 0.75-1" for a more premium feel
3. **Place brand elements**: Logo, company name, contact information
4. **Design header and footer**: Repeating elements for every page
5. **Create content area**: Clean space for letter body text
6. **Test with sample content**: Fill with realistic text to check spacing
7. **Export as PDF**: Save as a template that preserves formatting

### Multi-Page Considerations

For letters longer than one page:

- **First page**: Full header with logo and all elements
- **Subsequent pages**: Simplified header with just page number or minimal branding
- **Consistent footer**: Page numbers and confidentiality notice on all pages
- **Header continuity**: Maintain visual connection across pages

## Letterhead for Different Business Types

### Professional Services (Law, Accounting, Consulting)

- Conservative design with traditional layout
- Partner or principal names listed
- Registration and license numbers prominently displayed
- Confidentiality notices in footer
- Premium paper stock consideration

### Technology Companies

- Modern, clean design
- Bold use of brand colors
- Social media handles and website prominent
- Minimal, uncluttered layout
- Digital-first design (screen viewing optimized)

### Small Businesses and Freelancers

- Personal yet professional design
- Clear contact information
- Simple, cost-effective to produce
- Consistent with business card and email signature
- Adaptable to various document types

### Nonprofits and Educational Institutions

- Mission statement or tagline
- Tax-exempt status notation if applicable
- Accreditation logos
- Donor acknowledgment space (for certain documents)
- Accessible, inclusive design

## Printing Considerations

If your letterhead will be printed (pre-printed stationery):

### Paper Selection

- **Weight**: 24lb (90gsm) for standard, 32lb (120gsm) for premium
- **Finish**: Smooth, linen, or laid texture depending on brand personality
- **Color**: Bright white for modern brands, natural/cream for traditional
- **Size**: Match your design dimensions exactly

### Print Production

<TipBox title="Print Tip" type="warning">
If you pre-print letterhead on physical paper, work with your printer on color matching and test prints. What looks perfect on screen may not reproduce accurately in print. Always approve a physical proof before ordering a full print run.
</TipBox>

## Common Letterhead Mistakes

### Design Mistakes

- **Too much information**: Cramming every detail onto the letterhead
- **Poor logo quality**: Using a low-resolution or pixelated logo
- **Inconsistent branding**: Different colors or fonts than other brand materials
- **Cluttered layout**: Not enough white space around elements
- **Wrong dimensions**: Not matching standard paper sizes

### Practical Mistakes

- **Outdated information**: Old addresses, phone numbers, or email addresses
- **Missing elements**: No page numbers on multi-page documents
- **Inaccessible design**: Insufficient contrast or too-small text
- **Not testing in print**: Colors that don't reproduce well on paper
- **No template versioning**: Can't track which version is current
