## Choosing a Mobile Scanning App

### Built-in Options

Modern smartphones include basic scanning capabilities:

**iPhone (iOS)**: The Notes app has a built-in document scanner. Open Notes, tap the camera icon, and select "Scan Documents." It automatically detects edges, applies perspective correction, and creates PDFs.

**Android (Google Drive)**: Google Drive includes a scan feature. Tap the + button and select "Scan." It captures documents and saves them as PDFs directly to your Drive.

## Tips for Better Mobile Scans

### Physical Document Preparation

- **Flatten creases**: Iron or press documents flat before scanning
- **Remove staples**: They create shadows and uneven surfaces
- **Clean the lens**: A quick wipe of your phone camera lens prevents haze
- **Steady surface**: Rest your phone on a stack of books or use a makeshift stand

### Camera Settings

<TipBox title="Camera Tips" type="tip">
Turn off the flash — it creates glare and uneven lighting. Use the highest resolution your app supports. Enable HDR mode if available for better shadow and highlight detail. Most importantly, hold still or use a timer to avoid motion blur.
</TipBox>

### Post-Capture Enhancement

After capturing your scan, most apps offer enhancement options:

- **Auto-enhance**: Automatically adjusts contrast and brightness
- **Black and white**: Best for text documents — improves readability
- **Color mode**: Preserves color for documents where it matters
- **Crop and rotate**: Fine-tune the document boundaries
- **Sharpen**: Clarifies text that may appear slightly soft

## Organizing Scanned Documents

Once you've scanned your documents, organization is key:

### File Naming Convention

Use a consistent naming pattern:

```
[Type]_[Description]_[Date].pdf
```

Examples:
- `Receipt_CoffeeShop_2026-01-23.pdf`
- `Contract_VendorAgreement_2026-01.pdf`
- `Note_MeetingNotes_2026-01-23.pdf`

### Folder Structure

Create a logical folder hierarchy:

```
Scanned Documents/
├── Receipts/
├── Contracts/
├── Personal/
├── Medical/
└── Tax Documents/
```

### Cloud Storage Tips

- **Auto-backup**: Enable automatic upload to your cloud service
- **Two locations**: Keep copies in at least two cloud services
- **Regular organization**: Schedule time weekly to file and name scanned documents
- **Tagging**: Use metadata tags for cross-category searching

## Multi-Page Document Scanning

For multi-page documents, follow these best practices:

### Batch Scanning Process

1. **Scan all pages first**: Capture every page before making adjustments
2. **Maintain consistent orientation**: Keep all pages in the same direction
3. **Check page count**: Verify you've captured every page before combining
4. **Arrange in order**: Use your app's reorder feature to arrange pages correctly
5. **Combine into single PDF**: Merge all pages into one document

<ToolCardGrid slugs={["merge-pdf", "organize-pdf", "rotate-pdf"]} />

<TipBox title="Pro Tip" type="info">
Scan multi-page documents in landscape orientation when possible. This matches the natural orientation of most documents and reduces the need for rotation later. Hold your phone horizontally for wider documents.
</TipBox>

## Optimizing Scanned PDF File Size

High-resolution scans create large files. Optimize them for sharing and storage:

### Compression Strategies

- **Reduce DPI**: 150-300 DPI is sufficient for most documents
- **Convert to grayscale**: Black and white scans are much smaller than color
- **Remove unnecessary pages**: Delete blank or duplicate pages
- **Use PDF compression**: Dedicated tools can reduce size significantly

## Common Scanning Problems and Solutions

### Blurry Text

**Cause**: Camera shake, poor focus, or low light
**Solution**: Ensure adequate lighting, hold the phone steady, and let the camera autofocus before capturing

### Shadows and Glare

**Cause**: Uneven lighting or overhead lights
**Solution**: Move to diffused natural light, avoid direct overhead sources, and tilt the document slightly if needed

### Skewed Pages

**Cause**: Phone not held parallel to document
**Solution**: Use a scanning app with automatic perspective correction, or manually adjust the crop boundaries

### Large File Sizes

**Cause**: High resolution and color scanning
**Solution**: Use grayscale mode, reduce DPI, and apply PDF compression
