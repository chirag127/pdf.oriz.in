## What Are PDF Stamps?

A PDF stamp is a visual overlay applied to a page, typically conveying status information, approval, or branding. Unlike watermarks that span the entire page as a subtle background element, stamps are usually smaller, more prominent, and placed at specific locations such as corners, headers, or near signatures.

PDF stamps are implemented as stamp annotations, which means they can be added, moved, resized, and removed without altering the underlying document content. This non-destructive nature makes stamps ideal for document review workflows where multiple reviewers may apply different stamps.

## Types of PDF Stamps

### Approval Stamps

Approval stamps indicate the review status of a document. Common approval stamps include:

- **Approved**: Green checkmark or text indicating the document has been accepted
- **Rejected**: Red X or text indicating the document needs revision
- **Draft**: Indicates the document is a working version, not final
- **Final**: Marks the document as the completed, official version
- **For Review**: Indicates the document is pending review

### Date and Time Stamps

Date stamps record when a document was received, reviewed, approved, or processed. They can be static (fixed date) or dynamic (auto-populating the current date and time). Date stamps are essential for legal compliance, audit trails, and time-sensitive document processing.

### Certification and Authentication Stamps

These stamps verify the authenticity or certification status of a document:

- **Notarized**: Indicates the document has been notarized
- **Certified Copy**: Confirms the document is a true copy of the original
- **Quality Approved**: Used in manufacturing and quality management
- **Inspection Passed**: Indicates the document or item has passed inspection

### Custom Business Stamps

Organizations often create custom stamps that reflect their branding or specific workflows:

- Company logos and seals
- Department-specific approval marks
- Project or client-specific identifiers
- Confidentiality markings
- Payment received indicators

### Creating Custom Stamps

Custom stamps allow you to use your own designs, logos, or text as stamps:

**From an Image:**
1. Create or obtain your stamp image (PNG with transparency works best)
2. Use the PDF editor's custom stamp creation tool
3. Upload or select your image file
4. Name the stamp for easy identification
5. Save it to your custom stamp collection

**From Text:**
Some tools allow creating stamps from formatted text. Specify the text content, font, size, color, border style, and background to generate a stamp without needing a separate image file.

**From PDF Pages:**
An entire PDF page can be used as a stamp. This is useful for stamps that include complex layouts, multiple elements, or vector graphics that need to scale cleanly.

<TipBox type="tip" title="Image Format Tips">
For custom stamps, use PNG images with transparent backgrounds. This ensures the stamp blends naturally with the document content. Recommended resolution is 300 DPI for print documents and 150 DPI for screen-only documents.
</TipBox>

## Stamps vs. Watermarks: When to Use Each

Stamps and watermarks are both visual overlays, but they serve different purposes and have different characteristics.

### Stamps

- Small, localized annotations
- Typically placed at corners, near signatures, or in margins
- Indicate status, approval, or action taken
- Can be added and removed individually
- Appear as distinct visual elements

### Watermarks

- Cover a large area or the entire page
- Usually semi-transparent text or images
- Indicate ownership, confidentiality, or document status (draft, confidential)
- Applied to all pages uniformly
- Serve as background elements that do not interfere with content reading

## Digital Stamps in Business Workflows

### Document Review Cycles

Stamps play a central role in document review workflows. A typical review cycle might use stamps as follows:

1. **Draft**: Author submits the initial document marked as draft
2. **For Review**: Reviewer stamps the document to indicate it is under review
3. **Approved / Rejected**: Reviewer applies the final decision stamp
4. **Final**: Author stamps the approved document as final

Each stamp provides visual evidence of the document's progress through the review cycle, creating an informal audit trail.

### Legal and Compliance

In legal and regulatory contexts, stamps serve as evidence of processing:

- **Received**: Date and time stamp when a filing was received
- **Filed**: Indicates the document has been officially filed with a court or agency
- **Notarized**: Confirms the document has been witnessed by a notary public
- **Confidential**: Restricts access and signals handling requirements

### Quality Management

ISO-certified organizations use stamps to track quality management processes:

- Inspection stamps indicate items have been checked
- Approval stamps confirm processes have been verified
- Rejection stamps flag non-conformances requiring corrective action

## Stamps and Document Security

### Can Stamps Be Forged?

Standard PDF stamps are visual overlays and can be removed or replicated by anyone with a PDF editor. They do not provide cryptographic security or tamper evidence. For documents requiring authentication, use digital signatures instead of stamps.

### Stamps Combined with Digital Signatures

For maximum security and traceability, combine stamps with digital signatures:

1. Apply the stamp (approval mark, date, certification)
2. Digitally sign the document

The digital signature locks the document state, including the stamp. Any subsequent modifications—including stamp removal or alteration—will invalidate the signature, providing tamper detection.

### Audit Trail Considerations

If stamps are part of your audit trail, ensure:

- Stamps include the reviewer's name or identifier
- Date stamps record the exact date and time of review
- The PDF is digitally signed after stamping to prevent tampering
- Stamp metadata (author, creation date) is preserved

<TipBox type="warning" title="Security Note">
PDF stamps alone do not provide document security. They are visual indicators only. For tamper-proof documentation, combine stamps with digital signatures and document encryption.
</TipBox>

## Troubleshooting Stamp Issues

### Stamps Not Visible in Other PDF Readers

If stamps appear correctly in your editor but are missing when the PDF is opened in another reader, the stamps may not have been saved as standard annotations. Ensure stamps are saved as stamp annotations rather than temporary markups.

### Stamps Print Incorrectly

Verify that the stamp's print property is enabled. Some PDF editors allow annotations to be displayed on screen but excluded from printing. Check the annotation properties to ensure stamps are set to print.

### Stamps Cover Important Content

Reposition stamps to margin areas or corners to avoid obscuring document content. If you must stamp near text, use a semi-transparent stamp or a smaller size.

### Custom Stamp Image Quality is Poor

Low-resolution stamp images appear blurry when scaled. Use high-resolution source images (300 DPI or higher) for custom stamps, especially if they will be printed.
